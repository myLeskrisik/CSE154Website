To Pimp a Butterfly - Kendrick Lamar

  • Kendrick Lamar's Third Studio Album
  • Released March 15, 2015
  • Won a Grammmy for Best Rap Album
  • Genres: Hip Hop, Jazz Rap, Alternative Hip Hop

To Pimp a Butterfly album artwork by the artist Kendrick Lamar. Copyright belonging to Top Dawg Entertainment or its artist. Found on this site

This album is so beautifully complex, yet also very simple in many ways. The production by Sounwave, Thundercat, Pharell and many more, is second to none. Lamar talks aboutso many relevant topics, insitutionalization, self hate, the African-American communities views oncomplexion, all tied together beautifully with an ever growing poem at the tailend of most songs.

Give Up - The Postal Service

  • The Postal Service's Only Album
  • Released February 18th, 2003
  • Comprised of Ben Gibbard and Jimmy Taborello
  • Genres: Indie Pop, Bedroom Pop

Give Up album artwork by the artist The Postal Service. Copyright belonging to Sub Pop Records or its artist Al Columbia. Found on this site

One of my favorite sonically sounding albums, the beautiful synth beats by Taborello with their flickering notes and warm fun sound along withGibbard's delicate vocals and heartbreaking lyrics about loss are unmatched. The songtopics are all phenominal, The District Sleeps Alone Tonight about a breakup and tryingto deal with the change and the circumstances of the breakup, Such Great Heights talkingu about how relationships cause you to not care about everyone else and Brand New Colony speaking about the feeling of wanting to do everything for someone you love.

In The Aeroplane Over the Sea - Neutral Milk Hotel

  • Neutral Milk Hotel's Second Album
  • Released February 10th, 1998
  • Renowned Indie Album
  • Genres: Indie Rock, Folk Rock, Lo-fi

In the Aeroplane Over The Sea album artwork by the artist Neutral Milk Hotel. Copyright belonging to Merge Records or its artist Chris Bilheimer. Found on this site

Jeff Mangum, the lead-singer and guitar player for Neutral Milk Hotel, writes bizzarre and cryptic lyrics about Anne Frank, Stories of WWII, Adultery and depression on this album with an equally eclectic sound to match. Blown out fuzzy guitars, Mangum's odd singing voice all add up to one of the most unique albums.
